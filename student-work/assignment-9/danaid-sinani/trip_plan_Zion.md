When traveling to Zion National Park in Utah, consider flying into Las Vegas International Airport (LAS) or St. George Regional Airport (SGU), both of which offer convenient connections to the area with multiple airlines. The drive from the airport to your campsite can range from 45 minutes to an hour and a half depending on traffic conditions.

2. For accommodations, Watchman Campground is about 30 miles south of the park's entrance near Springdale. This campground offers basic facilities like restrooms and potable water. Lava Point Campground is located within the park itself, providing an easy access to scenic viewpoints and hiking trails. 

3. Fun fact: Zion Canyon, also known as the Narrows, is 15 miles long and up to 2,640 feet deep, creating a breathtaking natural wonder that offers awe-inspiring views along its length. If you plan on exploring this area, be sure to bring binoculars for an unobstructed view of the canyon's unique landscape and wildlife.

Day 1:
